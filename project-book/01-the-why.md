> This is the backstory. Every project exists because something was broken, missing, or painful.
> Explain the world before this project existed. Make the reader feel the problem.

## The Problem

*What pain point, gap, or frustration drove this project into existence? Be specific. Use real scenarios.*

## Who Feels This Pain?

*Which people, teams, or systems suffer from this problem? How often? How badly?*

## What Was Tried Before

*Were there previous attempts to solve this? Other tools? Manual workarounds? Why did they fall short?*

## The Breaking Point

*What was the final straw? A specific incident, a scaling problem, a business need? Every project has a moment where someone said "we need to build this." What was yours?*

## Why Now?

*Why is this the right time to solve this? New technology available? Team capacity? Market shift? This helps the reader understand the context in which decisions were made.*
